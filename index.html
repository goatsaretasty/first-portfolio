A little bit about me...

I grew up on the West Coast, traveled the country after high school and ultimately wound up in Portland, Oregon. There, I studied journalism and graphic design at a local community college, set behind the beautiful Mt. Hood. I became the news, design and associate editor of the campus newspaper and won first-place awards in the categories of best feature story and best writing at the 2006 Oregon Newspaper Publishers Association's collegiate conference.

I also worked tirelessly on extracurricular activities, from student government to honors work, and as a founding officer of the college's then-new Writers' Group.

At Portland State University, I switched gears to studying psychology, sociology, statistics, women's studies and biology. My broad interests earned me three bachelor's degrees, which was admittedly overkill. As with junior college, I was heavily involved in student government and other campus groups, having served as the Judicial Board's chief justice. Additionally, I was a head teacher's aide (as an undergraduate) in the behavioral neurosciences department, as well as a research assistant. In 2010, I became a Ronald E. McNair undergraduate achievement scholar and conducted original, grant-funded social science research.

Later, I would go on to study counseling and education, and I'd ultimately end up as a project manager for international ELA publishers.

But I've always had a passion for computer science, data and design, and I hope to one day call myself a professional front-end developer!

Jason M. White: technophile, PM generalist, aspiring front-end developer
